## 0. How to Use This Document

The PRD (`01_PRD.md`) decides *what* V47 says and *which* pages exist. This document decides *how V47 looks, sounds, and feels* while saying it. Every colour token, type choice, and voice rule below is downstream of one idea from the PRD:

> A CIO, CEO, or Ministry Director must conclude — within 60 seconds — *"boutique firm, senior leadership, real delivery,"* not *"freelancer."*

Where the PRD's §4 principle ("verifiable over impressive") is an ethical and copy constraint, this document turns it into a **visual and verbal constraint**: restraint reads as seniority; hype reads as junior. Premium is quiet.

If a brand decision isn't documented here, it hasn't been made — don't assume, ask (see §13, Open Questions).

## 1. Brand Strategy Foundation

### 1.1 The Positioning Thesis — read this first

V47's entire identity rests on one strategic observation:

**V47's visual and verbal peer set is not other AI agencies. It is the sovereign-grade technology institutions of the Gulf** — G42, Core42, Presight, Mubadala, ADQ, e& enterprise, ADNOC Digital. These organisations look *institutional, architectural, editorial, and calm.* They never look like a Silicon Valley SaaS landing page.

This single decision resolves 90% of downstream design questions. When in doubt on any visual or copy choice, ask: *"Would this appear on a G42 or Mubadala property, or does it look like a YC startup?"* Choose the former.

**Why this matters commercially:** your buyer (a government director, a bank CIO, a Big-4 partner) spends their day inside institutional-grade digital environments. A site that mirrors that register signals *"I operate at your level."* A site that mirrors startup aesthetics signals *"I'm below your level."* The aesthetic *is* the pitch.

### 1.2 Brand Pyramid

| Layer | V47 |
|---|---|
| **Essence** (2 words) | *Judgment, delivered.* |
| **Promise** | We take governments and enterprises from AI strategy to production — and every claim survives a reference check. |
| **Pillars** | **1. Executive advisory** (senior, peer-to-C-suite) · **2. Product thinking** (11+ yrs, systems not features) · **3. Working implementation** (prototypes you can click, not slideware) |
| **Proof points** | 11+ yrs product leadership · 5-yr national gov BI platform (120K+ users, 14 ministries, PM-level demo) · MENA banking AI/ML in CBUAE/SAMA-regulated environments · Abu Dhabi–licensed AI business |
| **Personality** | Senior · Precise · Candid · Systems-minded · Understated (see §2) |
| **Positioning statement** | *For governments and enterprises that are past the AI-hype stage and need to actually ship, V47 is a boutique AI consultancy that combines executive strategy, enterprise product judgment, and working implementation — unlike agencies that sell decks or dev shops that sell hours, V47 owns the whole path from strategy to production, and stakes its credibility on claims that survive scrutiny.* |

### 1.3 Brand Archetype

**Primary: The Sage** (the trusted advisor — clarity, judgment, truth over flattery).
**Secondary: The Maker/Creator** (the Sage who also *builds* — V47's differentiator is working prototypes, not just counsel).

The combination is the whole brand: *"the advisor who ships."* Avoid the Hero archetype (bravado, "we'll disrupt your industry") and the Magician archetype (hype, "AI will transform everything") — both are the generic-AI-agency traps §15 of the PRD warns against.

### 1.4 The Name "V47" — DECISION REQUIRED

A wordmark needs a story its founder can tell in one sentence in a room. Three defensible directions — **pick one, or supply the real origin; do not leave this undefined, and do not invent a story that isn't true** (per PRD §4):

- **A — Personal + precise:** *V* for Vignesh; *47* as a deliberate, specific number (specificity reads as engineering precision — "not a round marketing number"). Requires you to attach a true meaning to 47.
- **B — Vector 47:** *V* as vector/velocity (direction + speed from strategy to production). Positions the name around momentum.
- **C — Keep it abstract on purpose:** treat "V47" as a codename — deliberately undecorated, like an internal project or a product version. Institutional buyers respect restraint; an over-explained name can read as trying too hard.

**Recommendation:** Direction C for launch (say nothing, let it feel like a serious internal designation), with a private true meaning (A or B) held in reserve for when someone asks in person. **This is your call — flagged as Open Question OQ-1.**

### 1.5 The Anti-Cliché Guardrail

Three "AI-generated design" defaults to consciously avoid (they will make V47 look templated and undercut the boutique positioning):

1. **Near-black background + a single neon accent** (acid-green / electric-purple / cyber-cyan). This is *the* generic AI-startup look. V47 uses a dark foundation, but the accent is a **restrained brass**, used in hairlines and small type — never neon, never large fills.
2. **The AI-purple/cyan gradient hero.** Banned. If a gradient is used at all, it is a near-invisible obsidian-to-ink depth gradient, not a colour show.
3. **Warm-cream background + high-contrast serif + terracotta accent.** A common template look; also reads as a "Claude tell." Not V47.

The personality is carried by **editorial typography + an abstracted Gulf geometric motif + disciplined space** — *not* by a loud accent colour. Spend boldness in one place (§6.3), keep everything else quiet.

## 2. Brand Personality & Voice

### 2.1 Five Traits

1. **Senior** — writes peer-to-peer with the C-suite. Never eager, never salesy, never junior.
2. **Precise** — specific numbers, named methods, concrete deliverables. Vagueness is the enemy of trust.
3. **Candid** — states plainly what is *delivered* vs *in progress* vs *pitching toward*. This is the PRD §4 principle made audible.
4. **Systems-minded** — frames problems as architecture and operating models, not features and tools.
5. **Understated** — confidence without volume. The most senior person in the room is rarely the loudest.

### 2.2 Voice Principles (with do / don't)

| Principle | Do | Don't |
|---|---|---|
| Lead with the problem, not us | "AI pilots stall because no one owns the path to production." | "V47 is a leading provider of end-to-end AI solutions." |
| Claim only what survives a reference check | "Partnering with X on…" / "Delivered a national BI platform used by 120K+ users." | "Trusted by government leaders across the region." |
| Show, don't assert | "Here is the working prototype." (link) | "We have deep, world-class AI expertise." |
| Specific beats clever | "Prototype in 3 weeks; executive demo in week 3." | "We move fast and think big." |
| Plain over grand | "We help you ship." | "We unlock transformative value at scale." |

### 2.3 Banned Vocabulary

Never on the site, in a proposal, or on LinkedIn:
*leading · cutting-edge · revolutionary · world-class · game-changing · next-gen · seamless · synergy · unlock / unleash · empower · passionate · ninja / rockstar / guru · "leverage" (as filler) · "solutions" (as a noun for everything).*
Use **"transform"** at most once per page; it is the most exhausted word in enterprise AI.
No emoji in body copy or headings. (Acceptable only in informal LinkedIn captions, sparingly.)

### 2.4 Tone Spectrum

Tone flexes by context, personality holds constant:

- **Homepage / hero:** confident, spare, declarative. Short sentences.
- **Frameworks / Insights:** authoritative, teacherly, generous — this is where V47 gives away thinking to prove depth.
- **Case studies:** factual, structured, understated. Let the results speak; resist adjectives.
- **AI Lab:** candid and technical — including "lessons learned" and what didn't work. Admitting a limitation *increases* credibility here.
- **Contact / CTAs:** direct, low-pressure. "Book a discovery session," not "Don't miss out."

## 3. Logo & Wordmark

*(This section specifies the wordmark; final vector artwork is a separate deliverable — see OQ-2.)*

### 3.1 Construction

- **Wordmark:** `V47` set in the display serif (Newsreader) or a custom-drawn treatment. The **"V" and "47" may differ in weight** — e.g. V in a heavier optical weight, 47 lighter — to create a considered, engineered feel rather than a flat logotype.
- **Optional descriptor lockup:** `V47` with a small, tracked, uppercase descriptor beneath — `AI CONSULTING · ABU DHABI` — in the mono face. Use the descriptor lockup on the company profile, email signature, and footer; use the bare wordmark in the nav.
- **Monogram / avatar:** `V47` or a `V` glyph inside a square with the brand's geometric motif, for favicon, LinkedIn avatar, and social.

### 3.2 Clear Space & Minimum Size

- **Clear space:** minimum padding around the wordmark = the cap-height of the "V" on all sides.
- **Minimum size:** wordmark no smaller than 24px tall on screen; monogram no smaller than 32×32px (favicon 32/48 provided as separate raster + SVG).

### 3.3 Placement & Colour

- On **obsidian** (default): wordmark in `--text-primary` with the "47" (or a single accent stroke) in `--brass-500`. Use the brass sparingly — one accented element only.
- On **light** (documents): wordmark in `--text-ink`, accent in `--brass-ink`.
- Never place the wordmark on a busy image without an obsidian scrim behind it.

### 3.4 Misuse (do not)

Do not: stretch, skew, recolour outside the palette, add a drop shadow or glow, place brass-on-brass, rotate, outline, or set the wordmark in any face other than the display serif or the mono descriptor face. No "AI sparkle" iconography attached to the logo — ever.

## 4. Colour System

**Design intent:** a *sovereign, executive, engineered* palette. A cool obsidian foundation (calm, institutional, premium-dark) + a single warm signature (**brass** — Gulf-premium without being gold-kitsch) + one restrained functional accent (**azure**, for interactive/tech moments) + a full neutral scale + semantics. Warm brass against cool obsidian is the colour tension that makes the palette feel *considered*, not templated.

### 4.1 Dark Theme — Primary (all core pages)

| Token | Hex | Use |
|---|---|---|
| `--obsidian` | `#0A0D14` | Page background (base) |
| `--ink-900` | `#0E121B` | Deepest panels, footer |
| `--ink-800` | `#131824` | Cards, surfaces |
| `--ink-700` | `#1B2130` | Elevated surface, hover states |
| `--line` | `#262E3D` | 1px borders, dividers |
| `--line-strong` | `#333C4E` | Stronger dividers, active borders |
| `--text-primary` | `#EAEEF6` | Headings, body (≈16:1 on obsidian — AAA) |
| `--text-secondary` | `#A9B2C3` | Secondary text (≈9:1 — AAA) |
| `--text-muted` | `#6C7789` | Captions, meta, eyebrows (≈4.6:1 — AA; use ≥14px) |
| `--brass-500` | `#C9A65C` | **Primary brand accent** — hairlines, small type, key marks (≈8.5:1) |
| `--brass-400` | `#D8B978` | Brass hover / lighter |
| `--brass-600` | `#A9863F` | Brass pressed / darker |
| `--azure-500` | `#5B93E6` | Functional interactive — links, focus, active (≈5.5:1 — AA) |
| `--azure-400` | `#7DACF0` | Azure hover |

**Semantic (dark):** `--success #45B37A` · `--warning #D6A94A` · `--danger #E4715B` · `--info` = `--azure-500`. Use `--success` deliberately for the "verified / delivered" state on case studies; use `--warning` tone for the honest "in progress" labels the PRD requires.

### 4.2 Light Theme — Secondary (company profile, downloadable PDFs, print, optional article pages)

| Token | Hex | Use |
|---|---|---|
| `--paper` | `#FFFFFF` | Document background |
| `--bg-light` | `#F6F7F9` | Off-white page background |
| `--surface-light` | `#FFFFFF` | Cards (with `--line-light` border) |
| `--line-light` | `#E6E9EF` | Borders on light |
| `--text-ink` | `#0A0D14` | Primary text on light |
| `--text-ink-secondary` | `#414B5C` | Secondary text on light |
| `--brass-ink` | `#8C6E2E` | Brass darkened to pass AA on white (≈5:1) |
| `--azure-ink` | `#2A5FC0` | Azure darkened for light (≈6:1) |

### 4.3 The Brass Rule (critical — this keeps V47 out of the cliché)

Brass is a **seasoning, not a sauce.** Total brass coverage on any screen should be **under ~5% of pixels.** Permitted uses: a hairline under an eyebrow label, the "47" in the wordmark, a single key metric, an active-state underline, a chart's single highlighted series, a quote mark. **Forbidden uses:** large filled buttons, full-width bars, gradients, background washes, glows. If brass ever looks "gold and shiny," it's wrong — it should read as *brushed brass on dark stone*, restrained and expensive.

### 4.4 Gradients & Overlays

- One permitted gradient: a near-invisible `--obsidian → --ink-900` vertical depth gradient behind the hero. No colour gradients.
- Image overlays: obsidian scrim at 60–80% opacity to hold text contrast over any photography/screenshot.

### 4.5 Accessibility

All foreground/background pairs above meet **WCAG 2.2 AA** for their stated sizes; primary and secondary text meet **AAA**. Never convey meaning by colour alone — the "delivered / in progress" distinction must also carry a text label or icon, not just green vs amber.

### 4.6 GCC / Cultural Colour Notes

- **Green** carries positive regional and cultural resonance; V47's `--success` green is deliberately restrained rather than absent. Do not use it as a primary brand colour (too literal), but it's welcome as the "verified/delivered" semantic.
- **Gold restraint is a Gulf sophistication signal.** In this market, *understated* metallic reads as more premium than bright gold, which can read as nouveau. The brushed-brass tone (`#C9A65C`) is chosen precisely for this.
- Avoid palettes that read as any specific national flag or political colour combination.
- The sovereign-tech peer set (G42, Mubadala, ADQ) skews to deep, cool, jewel-and-metal palettes on dark or crisp white — V47 sits deliberately in that family.

## 5. Typography

### 5.1 The Tri-Typeface System — and why it *is* the brand

Three type roles, each mapped to one of V47's three pillars. This mapping is the typographic concept — not decoration, structure:

| Role | Face | Maps to pillar | Signals |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Display (serif)** | **Newsreader** | Executive advisory | Editorial authority, "we think" — the consulting-firm register, not the SaaS register |
| **Body / UI (grotesque sans)** | **Inter** | Product thinking | Clarity, modernity, precision |
| **Mono** | **JetBrains Mono** | Working implementation | Engineering proof — used for code, data labels, AI Lab, technical specs |

All three are free, variable, and Vercel/`next/font`-friendly. **Alternative single-family direction** (if you want a tighter, more "product" feel and are willing to drop the editorial serif): **Geist Sans + Geist Mono** throughout, with a serif only for long-form Insights. Recommendation: keep the serif — it's the strongest single differentiator from generic AI agencies, and it earns the "boutique consultancy" read the PRD demands. **Flagged OQ-3.**

**Fallback stacks:**
- Display: `"Newsreader", Georgia, "Times New Roman", serif`
- Body: `"Inter", -apple-system, "Segoe UI", Roboto, sans-serif`
- Mono: `"JetBrains Mono", ui-monospace, "SF Mono", Menlo, monospace`

### 5.2 Type Scale (responsive; base 16px = 1rem)

| Level | Face | Size (desktop) | Weight | Leading | Tracking |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Display XL (hero) | Newsreader | `clamp(2.75rem, 6vw, 4.5rem)` | 400–500 | 1.05 | -0.02em |
| Display L | Newsreader | `clamp(2.25rem, 4vw, 3.5rem)` | 400 | 1.1 | -0.015em |
| H1 | Newsreader | 2.75rem | 500 | 1.15 | -0.01em |
| H2 | Inter | 2rem | 600 | 1.2 | -0.01em |
| H3 | Inter | 1.5rem | 600 | 1.3 | 0 |
| H4 | Inter | 1.25rem | 600 | 1.35 | 0 |
| Body L | Inter | 1.125rem | 400 | 1.6 | 0 |
| Body | Inter | 1rem | 400 | 1.6 | 0 |
| Small | Inter | 0.875rem | 400–500 | 1.5 | 0 |
| Eyebrow / label | JetBrains Mono | 0.8125rem | 500 | 1.4 | +0.08em, UPPERCASE |
| Caption / data | JetBrains Mono | 0.8125rem | 400 | 1.5 | +0.02em |

### 5.3 Usage Rules

- **Serif for headlines and pull-quotes only.** Never set body copy in the serif — it reads as a blog, not a firm.
- **Mono earns its place** where there is real technical or data content (AI Lab, architecture labels, metrics, eyebrows). Using mono decoratively everywhere is a cliché; using it *as a signal of engineering depth* is the point.
- **Measure (line length):** 60–75 characters for body. Long-form Insights should not run full-width.
- **Numerals:** use tabular figures for metrics and case-study results so numbers align.
- **Sentence case** for headings and buttons (not Title Case, not ALL CAPS except mono eyebrows).

## 6. Iconography & Visual Language

### 6.1 Icons

- **System:** Lucide (already in the stack via shadcn/ui). Line icons only, consistent **1.5px stroke**, no filled/duotone icon sets, no emoji-as-icon.
- **Restraint:** icons support scanning, they don't decorate. One icon per card maximum. Never an "AI sparkle" / magic-wand icon — it's the single most overused AI cliché.

### 6.2 Imagery Policy

- **No stock photography** (PRD §15). Zero exceptions.
- **Permitted imagery, in priority order:** (1) real product/prototype **screenshots** — the strongest asset V47 has; (2) **abstract system/network/data-structure** graphics rendered in-palette; (3) **architectural / structural** photography (buildings, infrastructure — abstract, textural, obsidian-toned) used sparingly as section texture; (4) **one real, executive-grade founder portrait** (dark, editorial, high-quality) on About — not a casual headshot.
- **Treatment:** any photography is duotoned toward the obsidian/brass palette with a scrim, so images never break the palette. No full-colour lifestyle imagery.

### 6.3 The Signature Element — the Gulf Geometric Motif

The one memorable, ownable visual device: an **abstracted Islamic/girih-inspired line geometry**, drawn in `--line` / `--brass-500` at low opacity. Used as: section dividers, the hero network/atmosphere layer, loading states, and the monogram backdrop.

- **Why:** it is regionally resonant, differentiates V47 completely from Western SaaS, and — done as thin abstract linework — reads as *sophisticated and architectural*, not folkloric.
- **Guardrail:** abstract and geometric only. **Never literal, ornamental, or kitsch.** If it looks like a decorative border on a wedding invitation, it's wrong. It should feel like the wireframe of a structure, drawn in a single hairline weight. Spend the design's "one bold move" here — and keep everything else quiet.

### 6.4 Data Visualisation

- Charts on `--ink-800` surfaces; axes/gridlines in `--line`; labels in mono.
- One highlighted series in `--brass-500`; all other series in the neutral scale. Never a rainbow of colours.
- Every metric shown must be true and sourced (PRD §4 applies to charts too).

## 7. Layout & Grid (principles; detail lives in `04_Design_System.md`)

- **12-column grid**, generous gutters, wide margins. **Whitespace is the primary luxury signal** — resist filling it.
- **Spacing scale (8px base):** 4, 8, 12, 16, 24, 32, 48, 64, 96, 128. Sections breathe: ≥96px vertical rhythm between major sections on desktop.
- **Corner radius:** small and consistent (e.g. 8–12px on cards, 6px on buttons). Not pill-shaped (too consumer), not zero (too broadsheet-cliché).
- **Borders over shadows:** prefer a hairline `--line` border to heavy drop shadows for surface separation on dark — shadows read cheap on dark themes. Use at most a soft, low-opacity elevation shadow.
- **Alignment discipline:** everything aligns to the grid. Misalignment is the fastest way to look amateur to this audience.

## 8. Motion (principles; detail lives in `07_Motion_System.md`)

- Motion **supports the story; it is not the story** (PRD §6.1). Ambient, slow, purposeful.
- Permitted: a single orchestrated page-load reveal on the hero; scroll-triggered fades/rises (subtle, ≤300–500ms, ease-out); restrained hover micro-interactions.
- Forbidden: bouncy/springy consumer animation, parallax overload, auto-playing carousels, "AI typing" gimmicks, anything that delays the user reaching content.
- **`prefers-reduced-motion` respected** everywhere — non-negotiable quality floor.

## 9. Accessibility Standards (quality floor — not optional)

- **WCAG 2.2 AA** across the site (AAA for primary text, already met by the palette).
- Visible keyboard focus states on every interactive element (use `--azure-500` focus ring, 2px, offset).
- Full keyboard navigability; semantic HTML; alt text on all meaningful images.
- Responsive down to 360px mobile.
- Meaning never by colour alone (§4.5).
- Target sizes ≥44×44px for touch.

## 10. Bilingual / RTL Readiness (GCC — build this in from day one)

Even if V1 ships English-only, the brand and codebase must be **Arabic-ready**, because for an Abu Dhabi government audience this is a credibility signal:

- **Architecture:** use logical CSS properties (`margin-inline-start`, not `margin-left`), `dir`-aware components, and a layout that can mirror to RTL without a rebuild.
- **Arabic type pairing (reserved):** **IBM Plex Sans Arabic** for body/UI (harmonises with Inter's tone) and a Kufi-style display face (e.g. a premium Kufi) for headlines. Note that Arabic runs larger optically — reserve a separate Arabic type scale.
- **Wordmark:** decide whether "V47" transliterates or stays Latin in Arabic contexts (recommend: stays Latin, as a brand mark). Flagged **OQ-4**.
- **Content parity:** if/when Arabic launches, both languages must reach the same quality bar — a weak Arabic translation is worse than none.

## 11. Brand Governance & Application

The website is the hub, but the brand shows up everywhere the founder does. Consistency across these is itself the trust signal:

- **Company profile PDF** (the downloadable in PRD Phase 5): light theme, same type system, same brass restraint. This is often opened *before* the site by procurement — treat it as a first impression.
- **Proposals & decks:** built in the same design system (this is where `pptx` output should inherit these tokens). No separate "proposal template" that looks different from the site.
- **Email signature:** wordmark + descriptor lockup, mono descriptor, one brass accent line. No banner images, no social-icon clutter.
- **LinkedIn:** avatar = monogram; banner = obsidian + geometric motif + one line of positioning. Post copy follows §2 voice — candid, specific, no hype.
- **Governance rule:** any new artefact must be answerable to §1.1 (*"sovereign-grade or SaaS?"*) and §2.3 (banned words) before it ships.
